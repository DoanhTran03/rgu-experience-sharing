Phase 4

Wrapping Up & Submission

How to Meet the Rubric

Step 1 - Report Finalisation

The report must be 3,000 words +/-10%, professionally formatted, and follow the exact structure specified in the brief.

Checklist:

  • Cover page includes topic title, group name, all member names and IDs, and word count
  • Table of contents is present and accurate
  • All sections from the brief are present (Introduction, Critical Review, LESP, Methodology, Feasibility, Timeline, Conclusions from Statistical Analysis, References)
  • Word count excludes cover page, contents, references, and appendices
  • Appendix includes a log of any generative AI use

Formatting tips:

  • Use Word built-in heading styles (Heading 1, 2, 3) for consistency and to auto-generate the table of contents
  • Keep a consistent font, spacing, and margin throughout
  • Tables with mainly numeric content are excluded from the word count - use them where appropriate to save words

Step 2 - Referencing

This is one of the most penalised areas and one of the easiest to get right.

  • Use RGU referencing style throughout (check the RGU Library guidance)
  • References must be numbered and listed in the order they appear in the text
  • Every claim that comes from a source needs an in-text citation at the point of use - not just at the end of a paragraph
  • The reference list at the end must match every citation used in the body - no orphan references, no missing entries
  • In the presentation slides, references should also be numbered and appear on the relevant slide

Run through the report once just checking citations. It is very common to add a sentence late in writing and forget to add the corresponding reference.

Step 3 - Feasibility, Innovation and Sustainability Slide / Section

This section needs to cover three things:

  • Feasibility - explain why the project is actually achievable. Reference the availability of your data, the simplicity of your chosen tools, and the realistic timeline. If everything you need is open access and well-documented, say so.
  • Innovation - explain what is genuinely new about your approach compared to what the literature already does. This links directly back to the gaps you identified in the critical review.
  • Sustainability / Significance - connect your research to a broader impact. Reference a relevant UN Sustainable Development Goal explicitly. For finance and data science topics, SDG 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth) and SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure) are the most natural fits. One sentence explaining why the SDG applies to your specific project is enough - don't overstate it.

Step 4 - Presentation Slides

The slides should summarise tasks 1-4 within 15 minutes. Structure them to follow the report sections naturally.

Key things to check:

  • Every factual claim on a slide has a numbered reference visible on that slide
  • The narrative flows logically - each slide leads into the next
  • Visualisations from the statistical analysis are included and clearly labelled
  • The conclusion slide summarises the key findings concisely without introducing new content
  • Speaker notes or a script exists for every slide so timing can be controlled

Design consistency matters for the presentation rubric. Consistent fonts, colours, and layouts signal professionalism without the marker needing to read every word.

Figma is a good tool to design slides as you have more room to customise however you like.

Step 5 - Video Recording

  • Record using Microsoft Teams and upload to Microsoft Stream
  • Submit the link, not the file - check sharing settings so the marker can access it
  • Keep the recording to 15 minutes maximum - going over by more than 10% risks a grade penalty
  • Each speaker should introduce themselves by name at the point they begin presenting
  • Speak to the slides rather than reading from them verbatim where possible

Rehearse at least once as a group before recording. The main things that go wrong are timing, audio quality, and speakers talking over transitions.

Step 6 - Similarity Index and Final Checks

Confirm:

  • The R Markdown file knits cleanly end-to-end with no errors
  • The video link is accessible from outside your own account
  • Only one group member submits on behalf of the group
  • The self-and-peer review form is completed

Phase 4 Common Mistakes

Referencing only in the report and not on slides

The brief requires numbered references on slides too. This is consistently missed and was flagged in feedback. Every factual claim on a slide needs a visible citation.

Video recording submitted as a file rather than a link

The submission requires a link to Microsoft Stream, not the video file itself. Also check that the sharing settings allow someone outside your account to view it - many groups submit a link that only they can open.

Word count creep into excluded sections

Groups sometimes write dense, substantive content in appendices or footnotes to get around the word limit. The brief explicitly states this will result in a grade reduction. Keep appendices to logs and supplementary material only.

Similarity index left until last minute

Running a similarity check the night before submission doesn't leave time to fix anything meaningful. Run it at least a few days before so you can rewrite flagged sections properly.
